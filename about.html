The honest version

Why I started

I got serious about this because I didn't want to look back in ten years and wish I'd started sooner. High school gives me time that's genuinely hard to get back later — no full-time job, no mortgage, fewer responsibilities. I wanted to use it to build a real skill and a real business, not just talk about it.

My first attempt was promoting other people's digital products — ebooks and courses — through an AI-built website and daily social media videos. I posted constantly for over a week. The views were small and I made zero sales. Instead of treating that as a failure, I'm treating it as the first real data point.

The pivot

What changed

I realized I was spending all my energy promoting products that weren't mine, on a site I didn't fully control, with nothing to show for it that was actually mine. So I'm rebuilding around something more personal: my own site, my own voice, and eventually my own products — built from what I actually learn along the way.
